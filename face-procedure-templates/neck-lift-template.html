What is a Neck Lift?

Medically known as a platysmaplasty, a neck lift is a surgical tool used to correct wrinkles and sagging skin on the neck. These common signs of aging often create a condition that is less-than-lovingly referred to as a turkey neck. While a neck lift does address excess fat and skin, it also tightens a muscle in the neck known as the platysma muscle. This muscle becomes weaker and thinner as you age, which is often the underlying cause of a sagging neck. Added bonus—a neck lift can also eliminate the dreaded double chin.

Incisions made during a neck lift tend to leave some scars, even if they are small incisions. However, the location of the scar and who performs the procedure can go a long way towards minimizing them. Your plastic surgeon will do their best to be discreet about the placement of incisions in order to reduce visible scarring.
