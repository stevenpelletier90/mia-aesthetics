What is a Brow Lift?

As we age, gravity pulls on our bodies, including on the brow. This sometimes results in sagging, which can make you look sad, tired, or even angry. If the brow droops low enough, it can also interfere with your peripheral vision. In this case, a brow lift is often paired with blepharoplasty (a surgical repair or lifting of the eyelids). You may also develop two vertical lines between your eyes as your brow droops, commonly called the "11s."

Brow lift surgery elevates the brow, rejuvenating the face by eliminating sagging and lines, restoring your face's neutral expression. The procedure works well for those at all stages, from early signs of aging to severe brow aging. A brow lift can also improve your vision if the sagging in your brow obstructs it.

About The Brow Lift Procedure

Your surgeon has several surgical techniques to choose from when performing a brow lift, and the one he selects will vary depending on the extent of the procedure and your wishes. The differences between each technique revolve primarily around incision placement. Usually performed under general anesthesia, the four possible surgical techniques used during a brow lift include:

  • Endoscopic: During an endoscopic brow lift, the surgeon makes small, 1-inch incisions at your hairline. He then uses a small camera to help him visualize and manipulate his surgical instruments and work within your brow, suturing tissue in place. This procedure is minimally invasive, allowing for faster healing.
  • Direct/Mid-Forehead: Incisions are made above the eyebrows or within forehead furrows for a more direct approach.
  • Coronal: A single incision from ear to ear, often used for women with small foreheads.
  • Temporal: A minimal approach focusing on the outer third of the brow.

Brow Lift Recovery

Most patients require 10-14 days off work for initial recovery. Swelling and bruising are expected following the procedure and should be anticipated.

During recovery, keep your head elevated during the initial healing period to minimize swelling. You'll gradually be able to resume normal activities as healing progresses.

How Long Do Results Last?

Some patients experience results lasting a lifetime, though typically, results last 10-12 years. The longevity depends on individual factors including skin quality and aging process.

Ideal Candidates

Good candidates for brow lift surgery are:

  • Non-smokers
  • Generally healthy individuals
  • Experiencing brow sagging affecting appearance or vision
  • Have realistic expectations about procedure outcomes
