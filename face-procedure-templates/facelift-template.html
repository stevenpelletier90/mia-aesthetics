A facelift is a surgical procedure designed to remove and tighten excess skin while repositioning fat deposits and muscles that have sagged over time due to aging.

This procedure targets deep wrinkles, sagging jowls, and neck areas to create a more youthful and refreshed appearance.

The outcome varies based on individual needs and the extent of aging, with results that can take years off your appearance.

Types of Facelift Procedures

Full Facelift

The most comprehensive approach, combining multiple procedures to rejuvenate the entire face for dramatic results.

Lower Facelift

Focuses specifically on the mouth, jaw, and neck areas to address sagging in the lower portion of the face.

Mini Facelift

A minimal procedure with small incisions, best suited for patients with early signs of aging who need subtle improvements.

About the Facelift Procedure

A facelift is performed under general anesthesia as an outpatient procedure. The surgeon makes strategic incisions along the hairline and natural facial contours to minimize visible scarring.

During the procedure, facial muscles and skin are repositioned and tightened, excess fat is removed or redistributed, and excess skin is carefully trimmed away.

The goal is to create a smooth, natural-looking, and youthful appearance that enhances your natural beauty.

Facelift Recovery

Initial swelling and bruising are expected after facelift surgery. Your head must be kept elevated during the first week to minimize swelling.

Surgical drains may be used temporarily to prevent fluid buildup. Most patients can return to office work within 1-2 weeks, though strenuous activities should be avoided longer.

While you'll see immediate improvements, final results become fully visible over 6-12 months as all swelling subsides and tissues settle.

Are You an Ideal Candidate?

Good candidates for facelift surgery typically have:

  • Good overall health
  • Realistic expectations about results
  • Noticeable facial sagging or wrinkles
  • Loss of muscle tone in the face and neck
  • Adequate skin elasticity for optimal healing
  • Non-smoking status or willingness to quit before surgery
