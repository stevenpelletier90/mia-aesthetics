What Exactly is a Facelift?

Facelifts remove and tighten excess skin and reposition fat deposits and muscles that have sagged or fallen over time due to aging.

Facelifts fall into several categories: Full, lower, and mini lifts:

THE PROCEDURE

About the Facelift Procedure

General anesthesia is almost always used for full facelifts so that patients are unconscious and immobile during the procedure. Some mini facelifts can be done under local anesthesia with sedation.

The surgeon will make incisions necessary to access the targeted areas of the face. Through these incisions, the surgeon will adjust, lift, reposition, or remove tissue to enhance the appearance and contour of the face. Depending on the type of lift and the areas being addressed, excess skin may be removed to prevent sagging when healing is complete.

HEALING PROCESS

Facelift Recovery

Swelling, bruising and bleeding at the incision sites are typical after a facelift. Patients should plan to spend the first several days resting with their head elevated to encourage proper healing.

Initially, patients may have a surgical drain placed at the incision site to reduce swelling. This drain will be removed within a few days of the procedure. Bandages are also used for the first few days.

Most patients return to office jobs within one to two weeks following surgery, depending on the extent of the procedure performed and how quickly they heal.

Swelling will subside over the weeks and months following surgery and patients can expect to see final results anywhere from six months to one year following their procedure.

CANDIDACY

Are You the Right Candidate for a Facelift?

Ideal candidates for a facelift are in general good health, have a positive self-image and attitude, and have realistic expectations about what can be accomplished through surgery.

Your surgeon can give the appearance of turning back the clock with a facelift, but he can't actually stop the aging process. After your facelift, your face will continue to age naturally, but you will continue to look younger than you would have if you had not undergone the surgery.

There technically is no limit to the number of facelifts you can have in a lifetime. If you wish to have another procedure when you get older, that is possible.
