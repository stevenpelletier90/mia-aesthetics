Rhinoplasty, commonly known as a nose job, is a surgical procedure designed to reshape the nose and improve facial symmetry and appearance.

This versatile procedure can address both cosmetic concerns and medical issues, helping patients achieve better facial balance and, in some cases, improved breathing.

Whether you're looking to refine your nose's shape or correct breathing difficulties, rhinoplasty offers customizable solutions to meet your individual needs.

Why Choose Rhinoplasty?

Cosmetic Benefits

  • Change nose size or shape
  • Adjust nostril position and size
  • Improve facial symmetry
  • Enhance overall facial balance
  • Boost self-confidence

Medical Benefits

  • Correct breathing problems
  • Fix deviated septum
  • Address structural abnormalities
  • Improve nasal function
  • Reduce snoring issues

About the Rhinoplasty Procedure

Rhinoplasty is typically performed as an outpatient procedure under general anesthesia. The surgeon makes carefully planned incisions either inside the nose (closed technique) or across the tissue between the nostrils (open technique).

The surgeon reshapes the nose by removing or adding cartilage as needed. Cartilage grafts may be taken from the septum, ear, or in rare cases, from the rib to achieve the desired shape and structure.

The specific techniques used depend on your individual anatomy and desired results, ensuring a personalized approach to your nose reshaping goals.

Rhinoplasty Recovery

Initial recovery involves wearing bandages and splints to protect and support the nose during the early healing phase. Swelling and minor discomfort are common and expected.

While you'll see initial results within a few weeks, full recovery and final results can take up to a year as all swelling completely resolves and tissues settle into their new position.

During recovery, patients must be careful to avoid bumping or damaging the nose, and follow all post-operative care instructions for optimal healing.

Are You a Good Candidate for Rhinoplasty?

Ideal candidates for rhinoplasty typically have:

  • Good overall health
  • Realistic expectations about results
  • Dissatisfaction with nose appearance
  • Breathing difficulties or structural issues
  • Facial growth that has completed (usually by age 16-18)
  • Non-smoking status
