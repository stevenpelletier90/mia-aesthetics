What is a Tummy Tuck?

A tummy tuck, also known as abdominoplasty, is a popular procedure that removes excess fat and skin from the abdominal area.

Tightens and restores stomach muscles. Benefits include a smoother, firmer midsection with improved overall contour. Involves working with abdominal section from bottom of rib cage to hip.

THE PROCEDURE

About the Tummy Tuck Procedure

Usually performed under general anesthesia. Can be an outpatient procedure. Incision made from hip to hip, hidden beneath underwear/bikini bottom.

Procedure steps:

  1. Remove excess stretched skin
  2. Tighten remaining skin
  3. Perform liposuction in flanks
  4. Rejoin and tighten abdominal muscles
  5. Close incisions and place sutures
HEALING PROCESS

Tummy Tuck Recovery

Requires 1-2 weeks of strict rest and healing. Expect pain and discomfort, especially with muscle tightening. Pain medications will help manage discomfort.

Drain tubes may be inserted to prevent fluid accumulation. Compression garment required for several weeks. Most can return to work after about a week. Avoid vigorous exercise for 4-6 weeks. Swelling will gradually subside, revealing final results.
