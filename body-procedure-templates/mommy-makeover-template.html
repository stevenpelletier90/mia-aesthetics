Mommy Makeover

You adore your children, but you may not feel the same about the ways your body has changed since having them. If you want your pre-mommy body back, we can help.

The mommy makeover addresses body and skin concerns that often occur in women who have carried one or more babies. After having children, many women experience undesirable body changes such as weight gain, skin stretching, and breast sagging.

What is a Mommy Makeover?

The mommy makeover addresses all of these issues in a single surgery. A classic mommy makeover involves a tummy tuck and breast lift or augmentation, but it can be whatever you want. We can fully customize your procedure, combining whatever surgeries you'd like so long as your surgeon feels they're safe to do together.

The mommy makeover has no timeline and is enjoyed by new mothers and those whose kids are fully grown. In fact, you don't have to be a mommy at all to enjoy the benefits of a mommy makeover. The only guideline for the mommy makeover is that you should be done having children prior to the procedure. Getting pregnant after plastic surgery is safe for you and your baby, but the changes your body undergoes during pregnancy could alter your plastic surgery results.

Which specific procedures can be included in a mommy makeover?

Technically, any procedures you want that are safe to combine can be included in a mommy makeover. While the specific procedures included will vary depending on your individual needs and goals, some of the most common ones include:

  • Breast Augmentation
  • Breast Lift
  • Tummy Tuck
  • Liposuction and Lipo 360
  • Brazilian Butt Lift (BBL)

Mommy Makeover Recovery

The specifics of your recovery will depend on which procedures you elect to undergo. Generally, however, patients can drive in one week, walk five miles on a flat surface in two weeks, and run after four weeks.

Some women will feel ready to return to work after just one week, but most women need two to four weeks of recovery time before returning to work.

You should not lift more than five pounds during the first weeks of recovery.
