Labiaplasty, also known as labia reduction or vaginal rejuvenation, reshapes the external appearance of the genitalia for both cosmetic reasons and to treat any functional impairment.

The procedure often increases hygiene and symmetry of the labia, leading to increased comfort and confidence for patients.

The Labiaplasty / Vaginal rejuvenation procedure

On surgery day, labiaplasty patients will receive a mild sedative followed by general anesthesia.

The surgeon will make an incision on the labia major or minor and cut away the excess tissue, suturing the wound afterward. The surgeon then applies an anesthetic solution over the labia. The operation typically lasts one hour, and after about an hour or two of rest, patients are permitted to go home. Patients should arrange for someone to drive them home.

Types of Labiaplasty Surgery

There are five different ways to conduct labiaplasty surgery:

  • Edge Resection: Surgically removes excess skin from the edges of the labia minora.
  • Central Wedge Resection: Cutting and removing wedged tissue from the thickest segment of the labia minus.
  • De-epithelialization: Cutting the surface tissue of the labia minus.
  • Labiaplasty with Clitoral Unhooding: Resecting the clitoral hood and suturing it in a V pattern.
  • Laser Labiaplasty: Combines de-epithelialization with laser beams.

Labiaplasty Recovery

A patient can return to work a week after labiaplasty. Strenuous work should be avoided for at least a month, and intercourse should be avoided for six weeks following surgery.

On the first day post-op, the patient should stay in bed and arrange for a caretaker.

Are you the right candidate for Labiaplasty/Vaginal Rejuvenation?

Ideal candidates feel discomfort, pain, irritation or embarrassment from their genitalia and are looking for a solution that will improve both function and appearance.
