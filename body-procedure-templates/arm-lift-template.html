What is an arm lift?

Arm lift, also known as brachioplasty, is a surgical procedure that removes excess skin and fat from the upper arms, thus tightening skin and making muscle tone more visible.

THE PROCEDURE

About the Arm Lift Procedure

Performed under general anesthesia. Outpatient procedure. Surgeon makes an incision on the underside of the upper arm. Removes excess skin and fatty tissue. Incision length depends on tissue removal. Procedure takes 1-2 hours. May include liposuction.

HEALING PROCESS

Arm Lift Recovery

Surgeon bandages incisions. Patient wears compression garment. Pain managed with prescribed medications. Most patients return to work within a week. Activity restrictions for several weeks.
