Male Breast Procedures (Gynecomastia Surgery)

More formally referred to as gynecomastia surgery, gyno surgery is a surgical procedure performed to remove excess male breast tissue.

Impact

Although not a health concern, the formation of breast tissue in men (gynecomastia) can have an extremely negative impact on self-esteem, often causing men to avoid sports, intimacy, swimming, and other pursuits that make it difficult to hide the chest.

Candidate Requirements

  • Stop taking drugs/medications causing breast tissue growth
  • Wait until after puberty
  • Close to ideal weight
  • Generally healthy
  • Controlled chronic conditions
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol during recovery
  • Realistic expectations

These requirements ensure the best possible outcomes and minimize risks during the procedure and recovery period.

Procedure Specifics

  • Outpatient procedure
  • Approximately 2 hours long
  • Patients may have drains in incisions for 1-2 weeks
  • Wear compression garment for 6 weeks

The procedure is performed as an outpatient surgery, allowing patients to return home the same day with proper post-operative care instructions.

Recovery Timeline

  • Mild to moderate pain, controlled with prescription medication
  • Return to desk job in 5-7 days
  • Light exercise after 2 weeks
  • Full strength training after 6 weeks

Recovery is generally well-tolerated with proper pain management and following post-operative instructions.

Scarring Information

  • Surgeons aim to minimize and hide scars
  • Scars fade over time
  • Can be treated with silicone or vitamin E

Experienced surgeons use techniques to place incisions in less visible areas and provide guidance on scar care for optimal healing.
