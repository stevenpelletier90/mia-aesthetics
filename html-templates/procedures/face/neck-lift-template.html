What is a Neck Lift?

Medically known as a platysmaplasty, a neck lift is a surgical tool used to correct wrinkles and sagging skin on the neck. These common signs of aging often create a condition that is less-than-lovingly referred to as a turkey neck. While a neck lift does address excess fat and skin, it also tightens a muscle in the neck known as the platysma muscle. This muscle becomes weaker and thinner as you age, which is often the underlying cause of a sagging neck. Added bonus—a neck lift can also eliminate the dreaded double chin.

About The Neck Lift Procedure

The surgeons at Mia Aesthetics perform neck lifts as outpatient procedures under general anesthesia. You won't be allowed to drive yourself home because of the anesthesia, however, so you will need to have someone bring you to your surgery. A neck lift can take anywhere from one to four hours, so your ride will definitely want to bring a book!

Now that your ride knows what to expect, you should too. After you are prepped for surgery and drift off to sleep under anesthesia, your neck lift will proceed as follows:

  1. Your surgeon will make a small incision behind each ear and, depending on your needs, may also make a third incision beneath your chin.
  2. Through these incisions, your surgeon will tighten the neck muscles and the underlying tissue.
  3. Excess skin and fat will be removed as needed to recontour and shape the neck.
  4. Your remaining skin will be pulled taut over your neck and then your incisions closed.

Although a neck lift is generally very safe, there are always risks to major surgery. Choosing the right surgeon and following all pre-and post-operative instructions will greatly reduce the risk of complications.

Some potential risks may include:

  • Anesthesia risks
  • Bleeding
  • Temporary or permanent hair loss along the incisions
  • Facial asymmetry
  • Rare nerve injury that could cause weakness of the lower lip
  • Fluid accumulation (hematoma)
  • Infection
  • Scarring

Neck Lift/Rhytidectomy Recovery

  • Manage any discomfort with over-the-counter pain relievers. Pain after a neck lift is mild, and OTC medications are usually enough to keep you comfortable.
  • Sleep with your head elevated for a few weeks to reduce swelling.
  • Keep your neck straight as much as possible. Avoid turning or twisting it.
  • Avoid lifting heavy objects.
  • Wear shirts with buttons or zippers that you don't need to pull on and off over your head.
